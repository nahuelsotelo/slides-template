Leap of Faith

The most critical assumption you are making about your idea at this time, for which you have the least amount of evidence.

Users doesn't read the explanation lightbox of our fragment because they perceive the information there has no value for them.

  1. Hypotesis

    If we add the price and the amount of money users saves according to our estimation to the explanation, then they are going to spend 20% more time reading the information.

  2. Experiment

    Prototype of the explanation with price and savings. A/B test + User test.

  3. Behavior

    Measure how much time lightbox remains open. Investigate how Savings number impact in leads

  4. Target Metric

    20% increase in lightbox opened time.

  • Hypotesis -> measurable
  • Experiment -> let room for surprises (not just confirm)
  • Behavior -> No surveys!

Launch experiment!

  1. Actual Results

    Lightbox opened time was reduced instead of increased. Cars with higer savings amounts get more leads.

  2. Why?

    Users seems to check only the table with the numbers and skip the rest of the content.

  3. New Insights

    Users doesn't care about why they save money, only in the amount of money they save.

  4. Decision

    Add price savings under the price.
    Compare total savings vs saving porcentage.

  • Actual Results -> Record actual metrics. Atention to new insights
  • Why? -> Observ
  • New Insights -> Change / complement my initial assumption
  • Decision -> Next step
